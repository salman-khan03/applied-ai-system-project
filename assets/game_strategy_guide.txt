GAME STRATEGY GUIDE: Number Guessing Optimization
==================================================

BINARY SEARCH STRATEGY
-----------------------
The most efficient way to guess a number in range [L, H] is to always pick
the midpoint: M = (L + H) // 2. This eliminates half the search space each
turn. For a range of 100 numbers, binary search guarantees a win in at most
7 guesses (log2(100) ≈ 6.6).

Example:
  Range: 1-100, secret=73
  Guess 1: 50 → Too Low  → range becomes 51-100
  Guess 2: 75 → Too High → range becomes 51-74
  Guess 3: 62 → Too Low  → range becomes 63-74
  Guess 4: 68 → Too Low  → range becomes 69-74
  Guess 5: 71 → Too Low  → range becomes 72-74
  Guess 6: 73 → Win!

RANGE NARROWING
---------------
After each guess, update your valid range:
  - If outcome is "Too High": new_high = guess - 1
  - If outcome is "Too Low":  new_low  = guess + 1
Never guess outside your known valid range — it wastes attempts.

DIFFICULTY STRATEGY
-------------------
Easy   (1-20,  6 attempts): Binary search wins in max 5 guesses. Very forgiving.
Normal (1-100, 8 attempts): Binary search wins in max 7 guesses. One spare attempt.
Hard   (1-500, 5 attempts): Binary search wins in max 9 guesses. NOT enough!
  Hard mode requires trisection: split the range into thirds each turn.
  Trisection of 500 numbers: 500 → 167 → 56 → 19 → 7 → 3 → 1 (6 steps max).

COMMON MISTAKES
---------------
1. Guessing randomly instead of at the midpoint.
2. Forgetting to update the valid range after each guess.
3. Re-guessing a number that was already tried.
4. In Hard mode, using binary search (needs 9 steps) instead of trisection (needs 6).

SCORING TIPS
------------
- Win on attempt 1: +90 points (100 - 10*1)
- Win on attempt 5: +50 points (100 - 10*5)
- Each wrong guess: -5 points
- Minimum win bonus: 10 points (floors at attempt 10+)
- Optimal play maximizes wins per attempt.
